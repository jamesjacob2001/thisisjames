1. PROJECT DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

thisisjames is a personal homepage built with vanilla HTML5, CSS3, and ES6+
JavaScript. It serves as a simple, minimal introduction to James — his
background, interests, and the city he calls home.

The site consists of three static pages:

  1. Homepage (index.html)
     The main landing page. Introduces James with a short bio, a photo, and
     quick links to the two secondary pages. Features a typewriter animation
     as the site's original creative component.

  2. Interests (pages/interests.html)
     A page dedicated to James's personal interests: Boston sports, Formula 1
     motorsport, and music/DJing. Each interest has its own section with a
     short write-up.

  3. Boston (pages/boston.html)
     An AI-generated page covering a brief history of Boston, Massachusetts —
     the city that shapes many of James's interests. Content is organized
     chronologically by era.

All three pages share a unified navigation bar and footer. The visual style is
minimal and content-focused: clean typography, a neutral off-white palette,
and restrained use of borders and spacing to create structure without clutter.

Technology constraints:
  - Vanilla HTML5, CSS3, ES6+ only
  - No JavaScript frameworks or libraries
  - No jQuery
  - All JS written as ES6 modules (type="module")
  - Layout via Flexbox

2. USER PERSONAS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PERSONA A — The Recruiter
  Name:        Sarah
  Age:         32
  Role:        Technical Recruiter at a mid-size tech company
  Goal:        Quickly understand who James is, what his background looks like,
               and whether he might be a good fit for an open role.
  Frustration: Personal sites that are hard to scan, slow to load, or lack
               any real information about the person.
  What she does on the site:
               Lands on the homepage, reads the bio snapshot, checks the
               GitHub and LinkedIn links, and may skim the Interests page
               to get a sense of James's personality.

PERSONA B — Friend or Family Member
  Name:        Marcus
  Age:         21
  Role:        College friend of James
  Goal:        See what James has been working on, get a feel for the site
               he built, and maybe learn something about his interests.
  Frustration: Sites that feel too formal or corporate — he wants something
               that actually feels like James.
  What he does on the site:
               Browses all three pages casually. Reads the interests section
               and probably appreciates the Boston sports content. Checks
               out the Boston history page out of curiosity.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. USER STORIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Story 1 — Learning about James
  As a recruiter visiting the site for the first time,
  I want to quickly read a short bio and see links to James's GitHub and
  LinkedIn,
  so that I can decide whether to reach out to him for an opportunity.

Story 2 — Exploring interests
  As a friend or family member,
  I want to browse James's interests in sports, F1, and music,
  so that I can see what he's passionate about and feel like I know him better.

Story 3 — Navigating between pages
  As any visitor,
  I want a clear, persistent navigation bar on every page,
  so that I can move freely between the homepage, interests, and Boston pages
  without using the browser back button.

Story 4 — Discovering the Boston page
  As a curious visitor,
  I want to find and read the AI-generated Boston history page,
  so that I can learn something new while exploring the site.

Story 5 — Getting a first impression
  As a first-time visitor,
  I want the homepage to load quickly and feel visually clean,
  so that I immediately get a positive impression of James's taste and
  attention to detail.
