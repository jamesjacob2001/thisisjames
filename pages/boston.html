City Profile

Boston, Massachusetts

A brief history of one of America's oldest and most storied cities — written with the help of AI.

1630

Founding & Colonial Era

Boston was founded in 1630 by Puritan colonists from England, quickly becoming the largest town and most important port in colonial New England. Its natural harbour made it a centre of trade, and its streets — many following old cow paths — still shape the city today. The town meeting, a cornerstone of American democracy, was born here.

1770s

Cradle of the Revolution

Boston sat at the heart of the American Revolution. The Boston Massacre of 1770, the Boston Tea Party of 1773, and the battles of Lexington and Concord in 1775 all began or were sparked in and around the city. Paul Revere's famous midnight ride set out from Boston's North End. The city's role in the founding of the United States is unmatched.

1636 – Present

A City of Learning

Harvard, founded in 1636, is the oldest university in the United States and sits just across the Charles River in Cambridge. MIT, Boston University, Northeastern, and dozens of other institutions make Greater Boston one of the world's premier academic ecosystems. The city's student population shapes its energy, culture, and economy year-round.

1903 – Present

A Sports City Through and Through

Boston's sports culture runs deep. The Red Sox, who play at the beloved Fenway Park (opened 1912), won the first modern World Series in 1903. The Celtics are the NBA's most decorated franchise. The Patriots dominated the NFL for two decades. The Bruins are one of the "Original Six" NHL franchises. Sports here aren't just entertainment — they're identity.

Today

Modern Boston

Today Boston is a global hub for biotech, finance, and technology, while retaining its distinct neighbourhood character — from the Italian-American enclave of the North End to the Victorian brownstones of Beacon Hill to the vibrant arts scene in the South End. The city blends deep history with forward-looking ambition, a combination few cities in the world can match.
